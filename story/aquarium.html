Let me tell you a story of an aquarium. Each fish there was like a goldfish, in a tiny glass bowl of its own, afraid of an unseen cat prowling outside. Swimming in circles they chased an illusive change. But change is not a thing that can be found. It is a realization not a choice. There was, however, one fish which would often stare through the glass. One day, it swam to the surface and with all its might, jumped out. Bubbles rise to the surface of water and disappear. Fishes don’t chase them. They are hope.

Gasping for breathe as it fell back, it thought, “this is water.” It learned that there is something beyond the aquarium. Fishes don’t have a word for water. It continued to make higher, longer jumps. It was scared whether this effort will succeed. Burdened by the fear of regret that might come in the future, it had failed to realized that those tiny fins were getting stronger. They were taking another shape. It was the ten thousandth attempt, when it could stay in air and not fall. It fluttered its fins as hard as it was to breathe air. You wouldn’t know if you’re flying . It had grown wings. Next morning, filled with nothing but amazement, it spread its nascent wings and soared high. Most fishes swam by, some wondered how a bird could have lived among them this long. Others said they knew this one was different. They said the way it looked at the glass, they knew it would do something new.

The fish glanced down. In the grand scheme of things, the aquarium was tiny. The wind was cruel and sun was astonished. Once over the clouds, it shook off water. Then, like a habit, it shook again. There was no water now. Even if an aquarium is flying up in the air, for a fish inside it, there is no way to know it.

Read good stories. Etch silly dreams. Pursue them even if you chuckle looking back.
